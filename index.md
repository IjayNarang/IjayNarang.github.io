	      <h2 class="alt">I'm <strong>Pedro Paredes</strong> from <a href="http://cs.princeton.edu" target="_blank" >Princeton University</a></h2>
	    </header>	  
	    <p>I'm a Teaching Professor at <a href="http://cs.princeton.edu"
	      target="_blank">Princeton University</a> in the
	      Department of Computer Science.</p>

	    <p>
	      Before joining Princeton, I completed my PhD in Computer
	      Science in 2022 at
	      <strong>Carnegie Mellon University</strong>, where I was very lucky to be
	      advised
	      by <a href="http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~odonnell/">Ryan
	      O'Donnell</a>. Prior to that, I graduated from
	      <strong>University of Porto</strong> in 2017 with a Bachelor's and a Master's
	      degree, where I was also lucky to be advised
	      by <a href="https://www.dcc.fc.up.pt/~pribeiro/">Pedro
	      Ribeiro</a>.
	    </p>

	    <p>My primary research interest is in <strong>Theoretical
	    Computer Science</strong>. In particular I am interested
	    in: <br /> <u>Spectral Graph Theory</u>,
	      <u>Pseudorandomness</u>, <u>Coding Theory</u>, <u>Combinatorics</u>, <u>Quantum Information Theory</u>.</p>
